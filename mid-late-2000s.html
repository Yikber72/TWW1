Mid-Late 2000s: The Rise of Web 2.0

This period witnessed the rise of Web 2.0 — an era of glossy buttons, rounded corners, AJAX interactivity, and user-generated content. Social media began to shape digital design, and websites became sleeker, richer, and more dynamic.

Key Characteristics: Reflections, gradient buttons, tag clouds, AJAX, rounded corners, drop shadows, and soft blue/gray palettes.

Defining Design Elements (Mid-Late 2000s)

Layouts

Fixed-width centered layouts with full-page headers, footers, and tabbed navigation bars.

Color Palettes

Glossy blues, soft grays, gradients, subtle highlights and reflections.

Imagery & Graphics

Glass buttons, badge icons, mascots, and lots of rounded elements. Stock imagery was still common.

Typography

Mostly system fonts like Verdana, Tahoma, Arial — large text for headers and smaller navigation fonts.

Interactive Elements

Heavy use of JavaScript, AJAX for dynamic forms, tabs, and content sliders. Tooltips and popups emerged.
