Mid-Late 2010s: Material & Modular

The web matured into a sleek, responsive, mobile-first experience. Google’s Material Design set standards for layout, motion, and layering. Card-based design, subtle animations, and modern grids became default patterns.

Key Characteristics: Flat design 2.0 (with shadows), responsive frameworks, sans-serif typography, and mobile optimization as a must-have.

Defining Design Elements (Mid-Late 2010s)

Layouts

Grid and flexbox-based. Content organized into modular cards, often arranged responsively.

Color Palettes

Vibrant primary colors with neutral backgrounds. Inspired by Material Design’s bold contrast.

Imagery & Graphics

High-resolution photography, icons, and SVGs. Consistent and minimal visual styling.

Typography

Roboto and other Google Fonts widely adopted. Clear, legible sans-serif fonts became standard.

Interactive Elements

Hover effects, subtle transitions, and ripple animations. Buttons & inputs became highly stylized.
